CellChat & Its Limits

The dominant prior-generation cell-cell communication tool

What CellChat does

Groups cells by type → averages expression within each group → queries a curated ligand–receptor database → outputs a network graph of which cell types communicate via which pathways. Fast, interpretable, widely used.

Three fundamental limits

1. Averaging erases individual variation. A pool of 200 CD8+ T cells contains exhausted cells (PD-1 high, GZMB low) and effector cells (PD-1 low, GZMB high). Their average is a phantom cell that doesn't exist and doesn't behave like either real population.

2. No spatial information. CellChat typically runs on dissociated scRNA-seq data — spatial position is gone. It cannot tell you whether the interaction is happening in the tumor core or the margin, at 20µm or 200µm.

3. Database-constrained. Only interactions in the curated ligand–receptor database are visible. Novel mechanisms, metabolite-mediated signaling, physical contact effects — if they're not in the table, CellChat can't see them.

City analogy
CellChat: average menu of all restaurants in a city + average diet of all residents → conclude "there is food communication." AMICI: predict what a specific resident ate for lunch from which restaurants are within 50m of their home. If the prediction works, those restaurants influenced the choice.

Where CellChat remains valuable

It's not wrong — it's limited. For bulk datasets without spatial information, or for hypothesis generation across many samples quickly, CellChat is still useful. AMICI requires spatial data (CosMx, Visium, MERFISH); CellChat works on any scRNA-seq dataset. Different tools for different data.
