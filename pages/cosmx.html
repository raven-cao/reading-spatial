Technology & Methods

CosMx 6k Panel

NanoString's spatial molecular imager — the data source for this project

What it is

CosMx = NanoString's in-situ spatial transcriptomics platform. "6k panel" means a pre-designed set of 6,000 target genes selected to cover immune, tumor, stromal, and signaling pathways relevant to oncology.

How it generates data

Fluorescent probe pairs hybridize to target mRNAs directly in fixed tissue. Each gene is encoded by a unique fluorescent barcode sequence. The instrument scans the section in cycles, reading barcodes. DAPI staining delineates cell nuclei — the software assigns each detected mRNA signal to the nearest cell boundary. Result: counts per gene per cell, with (x,y) coordinates.

Why 6,000 genes instead of all ~20,000

Targeted panels are a trade-off. You sacrifice coverage of genes not in the panel but gain: (1) higher sensitivity for targeted genes, (2) manageable data size, (3) reduced cost. The 6k panel was designed to capture the relevant biology for tumor-immune studies — it includes key immune checkpoints, cytotoxicity markers, exhaustion markers, and tumor markers.

Limitation to keep in mind
If a novel interaction involves genes not in the 6k panel, AMICI can't detect it. The panel constrains the hypothesis space before analysis even begins.
