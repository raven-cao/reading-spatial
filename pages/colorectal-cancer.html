Cancer Biology

Colorectal Cancer

The biological context for this project

What it is

Cancer of the colon or rectum. Over 95% are adenocarcinomas — cancer originating in the glandular epithelial cells lining the intestinal wall. These cells normally form the absorptive and secretory surface of the colon; when their DNA replication machinery fails to correct errors, mutations accumulate, driving uncontrolled proliferation.

Why it's interesting immunologically

CRC is one of the cancers where the relationship between immune status and outcome is clearest. High immune cell infiltration (especially CD8+ T cells) correlates strongly with better prognosis. The MSI subtype responds to checkpoint immunotherapy; MSS does not. Understanding why — mechanistically, spatially, at the level of individual cell interactions — is the goal of this research program.

The three molecular subtypes in this project

MSS (microsatellite stable, ~85% of CRC): mismatch repair intact, lower mutation burden, immunologically cold. Does not respond to PD-1 inhibitors. Standard of care: chemotherapy ± targeted therapy.

MSI (microsatellite instable, ~15%): mismatch repair deficient, high neoantigen load, immune-hot. Responds well to pembrolizumab. First-line immunotherapy approved.

EMAST: elevated microsatellite alterations at selected tetranucleotide repeats — a distinct instability pattern, less well characterized, also in this study.

The central question
If MSI tumors have more immune cells AND those immune cells are more functional, is that because MSI tumors have more neoantigens (more targets), or because the spatial microenvironment is structured differently, or both? AMICI can begin to answer the second part.
