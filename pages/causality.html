Causality vs Correlation

Where AMICI sits on the evidence ladder

The ladder

  1. Correlation (CellChat) — A and B co-occur. Tumor cells express PD-L1; T cells nearby express PD-1. Maybe causal, maybe both driven by a third factor, maybe spatial coincidence.
  2. Predictive (AMICI) — B's state can be predicted from A's information. Stronger: if A didn't carry information about B, prediction would fail. But confounders remain: A and B might both be responding to the same microenvironment signal.
  3. Interventional (knockout experiments) — Remove PD-L1 from tumor cells; T cell PD-1 expression drops. Now we've established direction and necessity.
  4. Mechanistic — Know the exact pathway, timing, and intermediate steps. Rarely fully achieved.
AMICI's honest position
AMICI produces "estimates" not "measurements." Its outputs are stronger than correlation but not proof. The word "estimates" in the paper title is deliberate — the authors are claiming predictive accuracy and interpretability, not causal closure.

The detective metaphor

AMICI is a detective narrowing from hundreds of possible suspects to a handful of high-confidence candidates. The experiment (knockout, drug perturbation) is the court that delivers the verdict. Computational analysis exists to make experiments more targeted — not to replace them.

Why this matters for the CRC project

When AMICI finds that tumor cells are the dominant influence on T cell exhaustion in MSS tumors, that's a hypothesis with quantitative support — not a conclusion. The next step is a wet lab intervention: block that specific sender signal and see if T cell state changes. AMICI tells you exactly where to look.
